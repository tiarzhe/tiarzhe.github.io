Fitness
The three main metrics, activity, exercise, and movement, are an intuitive and quick overview of your daily activity. The circle will fill up as each metric reaches the set target.
Activity: Measure calorie burn during activity.
Exercise: Measure how long you've completed moderate-to-high-intensity exercise.
Movement: Measure how well you stand and walk throughout the day.
Activity
You can burn calories through light daily activities, running, cycling, or working out in the gym to help improve your fitness.
Exercise
A workout is a recording of minutes of moderate-to-high-intensity activity. 30-40 minutes of moderate-to-high-intensity exercise every day can help reduce the risk of chronic diseases.
Movement
Movement is to set an hourly activity goal and record the sum of the hours when the goal is reached. Standing and walking every hour can help reduce the risk of chronic diseases caused by prolonged sitting.
